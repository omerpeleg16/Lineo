# LINEO Landing Page Design Direction

## Design Goal

Design a premium product showcase website that presents LINEO as a polished AI product rather than an academic graduation project.

The experience should feel similar to modern product studio and SaaS websites:
- **Clean**
- **Elegant**
- **Minimal**
- **Technology-focused**
- **Visually refined**
- **Highly structured**

The design should emphasize the product itself through large visuals, motion and typography rather than long paragraphs.

---

## Overall Experience

The page should feel like browsing a premium digital product.
- Every section should have **one clear message**.
- Avoid crowded layouts.
- Create a calm reading rhythm with generous spacing between sections.
- The user should naturally scroll from one story to the next.

---

## Visual Style

### Keywords:
`Minimal` | `Premium` | `Editorial` | `Technology` | `Product Design` | `Clean` | `Confident` | `Refined` | `Soft` | `Modern`

The page should never feel corporate or overly "marketing". It should feel like a carefully designed product case study.

---

## Layout Principles

Use a modular grid. Content should be divided into independent cards and visual blocks.

Avoid long uninterrupted text sections.

### Prefer:
- Cards
- Split layouts
- Galleries
- Comparison blocks
- Timelines
- Statistics
- Process diagrams
- Large screenshots

*Every section should have a clear visual hierarchy.*

---

## Typography

Typography is one of the main visual elements. Use large headings and short supporting text.

> ⚠️ **Rule:** Avoid paragraphs longer than 3–4 lines.

### Recommended Rhythm:
1. Large Hero Headline
2. Medium section titles
3. Short descriptive copy
4. Key numbers
5. Large quotes
6. Large labels

*Typography should carry the page.*

---

## Whitespace

Whitespace is part of the design. Use generous spacing between:
- Sections
- Cards
- Images
- Text blocks

The page should breathe. Never try to fill empty areas unnecessarily.

---

## Cards

Cards are the primary layout component. Use cards for:
- Features
- Product decisions
- User quotes
- Statistics
- Workflow explanations
- Development process

### Card Design Criteria:
- Large internal padding
- Rounded corners (16–24px)
- Soft borders
- Subtle shadows
- Consistent spacing

---

## Imagery

The product itself is the hero.

### Whenever possible use:
- Real UI
- Animations
- Generated assets
- Videos
- Process visuals

*Avoid decorative stock imagery. Every illustration should explain something.*

---

## Motion

Motion should feel intentional.

### Animations should:
- Guide attention
- Reveal content gradually
- Support storytelling

### Use:
- Fade
- Slide
- Scale
- Parallax (very subtle)

*Avoid dramatic or playful animations.*

---

## Background

The page should not have a completely flat white background. Instead, use a very soft layered background inspired by the LINEO application.

### Characteristics:
- Very subtle radial gradients
- Soft teal glow
- Light gray-blue base
- Almost invisible transitions
- Large blurred light areas

The background should create depth while remaining unobtrusive. It should feel like the interface is floating above a calm illuminated surface. Each section can introduce slight background variations while maintaining the same visual language.

---

## Product Showcase

The product should always receive the largest visual emphasis.
- Screenshots should appear large.
- Videos should be embedded naturally into the layout.
- Generated assets should be displayed like a design system rather than a gallery.
- Show relationships between elements instead of isolated images.

---

## Content Style

- Less text.
- More visuals.
- One idea per section.

Explain through diagrams, comparisons and images whenever possible. Every section should answer one question:
- *What problem exists?*
- *How does LINEO solve it?*
- *What can it generate?*
- *How does it work?*
- *What were the design decisions?*
- *What impact did it create?*

---

## UI Inspiration

The page should combine characteristics of:
- Modern SaaS websites
- Product case studies
- Design studio portfolios
- AI product launches

It should feel polished enough that visitors immediately recognize it as a professional product presentation.

---

## Storytelling Through Scroll

The landing page should not feel like a sequence of independent sections. Instead, each section should naturally transition into the next.

### Whenever possible, create continuity using:
- Shared visual elements
- Connecting animations
- Expanding layouts
- Morphing graphics
- Scroll-triggered transitions

The experience should feel like watching the product unfold rather than reading a document. Scrolling should reveal the story progressively.
